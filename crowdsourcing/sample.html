Understanding Different Types of Edits

Instructions

You will be given two versions of a sentence: before and after editing. It is your job to determine one or more categories that the sentence fits into.

For each version of the sentence, the red text indicates words that were removed, green text represents words that were added, and black words are words that were not changed.

There are definitions of each category, examples of each category with an explanation, and then 6 questions to answer.

Not knowing the answer is ok; part of this is understanding why certain categories are harder to understand than others.

These sentences come from PDFs. In order to make sentence more uniform, mathematical equations such as \(a \ne 0\) or \( \Sigma=\{\vec v=(v_1,v_2) \in \mathbb{R}^2\} \) have been replaced with [MATH].

Good luck!

Category Definitions

Edits that change meaning

Elaboration: Adds new information to a sentence such as a claim, piece of evidence, or explanation.

Simplification: Removes information such as a claim, a piece of evidence, or an explanation from the sentence.

Edits that don't change meaning

Correction: Fixes spelling, grammar or formatting without changing the meaning or specifity of a sentence. This includes changing a sentence to match conventions (Fig. instead of Figure, one-dimensional instead of one dimensional).

Reading flow: Makes or changes connections between different parts of the paper, changing the order in which a reader would move through the document. This includes adding or removing a reference to a figure, equation, or other section of a paper like an appendix or conclusion.

Word usage: Changes language, tone or style without changing the meaning of the sentence.

Edits that can't be labeled with another category

Domain knowledge: You need further understanding of the subject to know what categories best fit.

PDF required: The original PDF contains information not shown in the sentence, which is needed to pick out categories for an example.

Other: None of these labels fit.
